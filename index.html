Semantic elements summary

So, what are Semantic elements? HTML semantic elements describe its meaning to the browser and developer. It tells us about its contents.

Semantic elements have a few things going for them. They are:

  • Accessible: they help assistive technologies and developers read and interpret a webpage.
  • Searchable: they help computers make sense of the content.
  • Internationalized: they help non-English speakers understand the document.
  • Interoperable: they help other developers understand the structure of the page.

When building a webpage, developers use a combination of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. HTML is used for the basic outline; the organization and content of a webpage. I like to think of it as the foundation of a webpage. The semantic elements are what is used to build this foundation. They are the base layer; the contents of a webpage. CSS and JavaScript is used on top it to make the webpage “prettier”.
